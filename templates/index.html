About GatoHKG

Founded in Manchester in 2024 by a HK family who immigrated to the UK in 2022. GatoHKG is a icon of Hong Kong-style cakes, and Pastries delights, and authentic Asian cuisine. Rooted in a passion for food and inspired by the rich culinary traditions of Hong Kong, our family started this journey to share the vibrant flavors we grew up enjoying with the people of the UK.

At GatoHKG, we bring the warmth and nostalgia of home-cooked meals and freshly baked goods, offering a unique fusion of traditional recipes and a modern twist. From buttery pineapple buns to delicate egg tarts, and hearty Asian comfort dishes, we aim to introduce and delight our customers with the taste of Asia.

Our mission is simple: to bring people together through food, bridging cultures and creating memories that linger long after the last bite. Come and taste the heart of Asia right here in the UK, where every dish tells a story of tradition, family, and love !

Scones

GataHKG's scones bring a classic British treat to your table—golden-brown, flaky, and buttery. Perfect for afternoon tea or breakfast, they feature a crisp exterior with tender, fluffy layers inside. Served warm with clotted cream and jam, each bite is a comforting, satisfying delight.

Muffins

Soft, fluffy, and bursting with unique flavors inspired by Asian cuisine. Perfect for any time of the day, our muffins are a blend of tradition and creativity, crafted to bring a taste of home to the UK. Taste the difference in every bite!

Swiss Rolls

Swiss rolls are a Hong Kong bakery favorite—light, fluffy, and mildly sweet. Featuring a spiral of airy sponge cake and creamy filling, each bite melts in your mouth. Made with rich ingredients like butter and condensed milk, they pair perfectly with tea or coffee for a delightful treat.


Egg Tarts

The Hong Kong-style egg tart is a beloved pastry that features a smooth, creamy custard center nestled in a flaky or crumbly pastry shell. Drawing inspiration from Portuguese and British influences, this treat became a local staple, often found in bakeries, cha chaan tengs, and dim sum restaurants. Its delicate balance of sweetness and buttery richness makes it a quintessential part of Hong Kong’s culinary identity.

Turnip Cakes

Turnip cake, or "lo bak go," is a savory dim sum favorite made from shredded daikon radish, rice flour, and a mix of ingredients like dried shrimp, Chinese sausage, and mushrooms. Often served in rectangular slices, it’s a comforting dish that showcases the umami-packed simplicity of traditional Cantonese cuisine. Commonly enjoyed during dim sum meals or festive occasions like Chinese New Year, it’s a must-try for those exploring Hong Kong's culinary traditions.

Red Bean Pudding Cakes

Red bean pudding, known locally as "put chai ko," is a classic Hong Kong street snack that offers a nostalgic taste of childhood for many locals. Made from glutinous rice flour, sugar, and red beans, the pudding is steamed in small bowls or aluminum cups until firm and springy. The subtly sweet and chewy texture, paired with the earthy flavor of red beans, makes this treat a simple yet satisfying dessert that remains a favorite across generations.
